Who We Are
Evidence Action scales proven development solutions to benefit millions of people
around the world. We fill the gap between knowing “what works” and having impact at
scale. We implement cost-effective interventions whose efficacy is backed by substan-
tial rigorous evidence. We identify innovative, appropriate financing mechanisms and

build best practice operational models. We voraciously self-evaluate, learn, and improve
our models for scaling with a commitment to transparency on progress, impact, and
value for money. Consider this styleguide a set of evolving guidelines that will help you

Instructions on Installing Fonts in Windows and Mac
Windows:
1. Open Fonts by clicking the Start button, clicking Control Panel, clicking Appearance
and Personalization, and then clicking Fonts.
2. Click File, and then click Install New Font. If you don’t see the File menu, press ALT.
3. In the Add Fonts dialog box, under Drives, click the drive where the font that you
want to install is located.

4. Under Folders, double-click the folder containing the fonts that you want to add.
5. Under List of fonts, click the font that you want to add, and then click Install.

• Lucid and Accessible

• Smart
• Entrepreneurial
• Expert without being condescending

• Optimistic, yet pragmatic

Writing for Evidence Action
Ethical guidelines for the dignified depiction of people:
Copyright Conditions
•
Evidence Action takes great pride in depicting our customers and the people we work with
in any country with dignity and respect, and with agency as human beings. Unlike other
charities, we do not want to invoke a sense of pity or depict our customers or people we
work with any less than the full human beings that they are.
•
Photographers must respect their subjects, preserve human dignity, and take into consider-
ation whether their photo subjects may experience negative consequences of having their
photo taken and used.
•
Evidence Action employees are expected to obtain consent from all photo subjects before
the picture is taken.
